Storytime Magic.  Group story-writing.  We wrote about people named Bob, Whales, Fairied, little fish... anything that popped into our heads.

Somewhere around here was June 8, 2007.  My 13th Birthday.  I got a Wii, and probably the most life-changing game for me:  THE LEGEND OF ZELDA: TWILIGHT PRINCESS.

And thus begun the most action-packed last few weeks of school... ever.

Almost immediately, I liked the game.  So I incorporated it into my latest invention.  Twilight Princess + Storytime Magic = (drumroll here) The Legend of Ilia.

Prolouge

John ran down to the spring.  Will was sprinting behind him, but was gradually falling further and further back.

'Hey, John, slow down!'  Will was only ten, four years younger than John.

John did not slow down, but he did glance behind his shoulder.  He looked at Will for a second, then turned his head forward again.  'She has my horse, Will.  My horse.'

As John passed the spring, Will slowed to a walk.  John ran on, without even glancing back.


'Come on, Elli.  Give me back my horse,' John said for the twentieth time.

'No,' Elli said.  It was all she had said to John that day.

'Please?'

'No.'

'Why not?'

Elli said nothing, but pointed to a small wound on the horse's leg.

'That wasn't my fault.'

'I don't care whose fault it was.  I'm not letting you on this horse.'

'Just give her to me.'

'Why should I?'

'Because she's my horse!'

'So?'

'So, you're kidnapping my horse.  How about I go tell your dad the happy news?'

'Fine.  Take her.  But if it happens again, I won't be so forgiving.'

'Whatever.'

'I mean it.'

'I'm sure you do.'

Elli turned around and walked off.
